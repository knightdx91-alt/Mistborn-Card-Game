# Mistborn: The Deckbuilding Game — Rules Reference

> Publisher: Brotherwise Games, LLC (© 2024). Designed by John D Clair.
> Based on Brandon Sanderson's Mistborn® Saga. Source: official Rulebook V1.
> This is a condensed reference for building against the game; it is not a
> replacement for the official rulebook.

## Overview

A competitive deckbuilder for 2–4 players (with a solo/co-op mode against the
Lord Ruler). Each player is a Mistborn who ingests and **Burns** metals to fuel
Allomantic powers. You start with a weak 10-card deck and build a stronger one
by buying from a shared Market.

## Components

- 4 Character cards, 4 Starter decks (10 cards each)
- 82 Market cards, 8 Mission cards
- 36 Lord Ruler Challenge cards + Lord Ruler card & Dominance track
- 16 Atium tokens, 4 Player Training tracks
- 4 Health dials (+ tracking cubes), 4 sets of 8 metal tokens (32 total)
- Lord Ruler dial & 8 tracking cubes, 14 Boxing tokens, Target standee

## Ways to Win (competitive)

1. **Missions** — be first to the top of all **3** Mission tracks.
2. **Elimination** — be the last player alive.
3. **Confrontation** — play **4 Atium** onto the *Confrontation* card.

## Setup (per player)

- Random Character card; Training track + tracking cube (on step 1).
- 8 metal tokens placed above the Training track.
- Starting deck: 4 character-specific Training cards + 6 Funding cards, shuffled.
- Health dial set to 36.
- Shuffle Market deck; reveal top 6 as the **Market**.
- Reveal 3 random Missions (of 8); each player places a cube on each.
- First-player catch-up bonuses: 2nd player +2 health, 3rd +4, 4th +4 & a Boxing.
- In 3+ player games the last player (clockwise from first) gets the **Target**.
- Each player draws a starting hand of 5.

## Core Deckbuilder Rules

- Each player has: hand of 5, facedown deck, faceup discard, cards in play.
- You may inspect your own hand and discard, never your deck.
- Gained cards go to the **discard pile** unless stated otherwise.
- End of turn: move all cards in play (except Allies) + unplayed hand to discard,
  then draw 5. Empty deck → shuffle discard into a new deck.
- Eliminated cards go to a shared **eliminated pile** (visible to all).

## Metals & Burning

The 8 metal tokens are the fuel for Allomancy.

- **Burning**: to activate most Action abilities, Burn a metal — move the token
  from the Training track onto the card. Return it at end of turn.
- **Burn limit**: starts at **1/turn**, rises up the Training track to **4**.
- **Cards as metals**: play a card sideways (showing its metal vial) to power
  another card that calls for one of its two paired metals. This does **not**
  count against your burn limit — unlimited per turn.
- **Flaring**: to exceed the burn limit, flip an unused token and use it; it
  stays flipped (spent) until **Refreshed**. Flaring counts as Burning for
  effects. Unlimited Flares per turn.
- **Refreshing**: discard a card from hand to flip back a Flared token matching
  one of the card's paired metals. Some cards/Missions refresh any Flared metal.
- **Atium**: the most powerful metal, not available at start. Gained from the
  Training track / Missions. Single-use wild — powers Atium cards or substitutes
  for any metal (counts as *both* Atium and that metal). Cannot be Flared.
- **Burn for no effect**: you may Burn/Flare into an empty space to trigger
  Savant/Ally/character effects that check "if you Burn X".

## The 8 Metals (pairings & keywords)

| Metal  | Focus                                   | Keyword  | Keyword effect |
|--------|-----------------------------------------|----------|----------------|
| Steel  | Combat, Coins, Market manipulation      | PUSH     | Eliminate any Market card (replace immediately) |
| Iron   | Combat, future-turn setup               | PULL     | Move cards from discard to top of deck |
| Pewter | Combat + Healing                        | DEFENDER | Damage can't target you/non-Defender allies while out |
| Tin    | Mission Points + Coins                  | SENSE    | Stop a player advancing on Mission tracks this turn |
| Bronze | Mission Points, use Market abilities    | SEEK     | Use the top effect of any Market Action up to a cost |
| Copper | Healing, damage blocking                | CLOUD    | Prevent/reduce damage to a player or their allies |
| Zinc   | Coins, use Allies without Burning       | RIOT     | Activate any Ally's top effect free (once) |
| Brass  | Mission Points + Coins, card removal    | SOOTHE   | Eliminate your own card from hand/discard/play |

(Metal pairings: Steel/Iron, Pewter/Tin, Bronze/Copper, Zinc/Brass.)

## Cards

**Action cards** (vertical): name, cost, primary metal + effect, optional
secondary abilities requiring additional metals (top ability must fire first),
metal pairing (for use-as-metal / refresh), optional **Savant** ability (only
when used as a metal), optional **off-turn** ability (discard to trigger).

**Ally cards** (horizontal): played above your character, persist until defeated
(then to owner's discard). Have a Defense value; give an effect whenever you Burn
their associated metal. Can't be used as metals or to refresh.

**Funding cards**: starter-deck only; give 1 Coin, no metal cost.

**Boxings**: buy for 2 Coins; discard later for 1 Coin. Never expire, unlimited.

## Character Card & Training Track

At the start of each turn, move one step up the Training track. Rewards:

- **+1 Burn** (extra metal per turn; up to 4 total).
- **Unlock a character ability** (usable every turn once unlocked).
- **Atium token** (single use).
- **Continuous Atium** (end of track): gain an Atium each time you go up a
  Mission track (incl. start of turn).

Every character shares two unlockable abilities: (1) once/turn, when you buy an
Action from the Market you may eliminate it and immediately do its top effect
free; (2) an Atium effect that triggers whenever you Burn Atium.

## Missions

Play with 3 of 8. Each track needs **12 Mission Points** to top out. Gain points
from effects (split or stack them). Tracks have mid-track rewards, extra
first-player rewards, and a top reward (one-time or permanent). First to top a
track is permanently "highest" on it.

## Combat

- Damage points are used at end of turn; may split between Allies and players.
- Allies die only if dealt damage ≥ their Defense in one attack (no partial).
- **DEFENDER** allies must be killed before their owner/other allies can be hit.
- **2-player**: at end of turn, optionally send all combat at the opponent.
- **3–4 player**: the **Target** mechanic — you must aim damage at the Target
  holder (after attacking allies). After they take damage they may pass the
  Target. Holding the Target means you damage *all* opponents on your turn.
- **Healing** raises your dial (max 40 health). At 0 health you're eliminated.

## Turn Structure

1. Move one space up the Training track.
2. In any order, any number of times: play cards; Burn/Flare metals; use cards
   as metals; discard to refresh; activate Ally/character abilities; move up
   Mission tracks; buy from Market; attack Allies; attack players (then Target
   may move if its holder took damage).
3. Discard cards in play (except Allies) and remaining hand.
4. Draw 5.

## Solo & Co-op (vs. the Lord Ruler)

- No Target; no starting bonuses. Lord Ruler starts at 48 health with a 36-card
  deck and a **Dominance track** (power 1→6).
- After each player's turn, flip the top Lord Ruler card:
  - **Adversaries**: minions placed in front of you with ongoing/end-of-turn
    negative effects; have 1–3 shields (destroy left→right, damage ≥ shield
    value; "X" = current Dominance). Any player can attack any Adversary.
  - **Edicts**: resolve 4 effects in order — Dominance Up, an additional effect,
    Lord Ruler healing (+10 per uncompleted Mission, max 48), and Market clearing
    (eliminate 2 Market cards).
- **Co-op**: players don't attack each other and may protect each other (CLOUD).
- **End of game** (solo/co-op): Lord Ruler deck runs out (players lose) · all
  players eliminated (lose) · Lord Ruler hits 0 (win) · 4 Atium on Confrontation
  (win). Reaching the top of all Missions does **not** end the game here.
