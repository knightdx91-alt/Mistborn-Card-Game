# Mistborn: House War — Rules Reference

> Publisher: Crafty Games, LLC. Game Design: Kevin Wilson. Based on Brandon
> Sanderson's Mistborn® Saga (set during *The Final Empire*). Source: official
> rulebook (med-res PDF). This is a condensed reference, not a replacement for
> the official rulebook. Note: this is the **negotiation board game**, distinct
> from *Mistborn: The Deckbuilding Game* and the *Mistborn Adventure Game* (RPG).

## Overview

A semi-cooperative **negotiation / deal-making board game** for 3–5 players. Each
player leads a Great Noble House of Luthadel under the Lord Ruler. Houses must
jointly solve **Problems** threatening the Final Empire — but resources are
scarce, so solving them requires brokering (and breaking) deals. Solving a
Problem earns **Favor** (from the Lord Ruler); eruptions inflict **Disgrace**.
The twist: how you want to win flips depending on whether the Empire survives.

## Win Conditions

Score = **total Favor − total Disgrace**. The game ends immediately when:

1. **Vin Problem solved** → Final Empire saved → **highest score wins**.
2. **Vin Problem erupts** with **Unrest ≤ 7** → Empire barely saved → **highest score wins**.
3. **Unrest ≥ 8** (any time) → Empire **collapses** → **lowest score wins**.

So a runaway Favor lead is dangerous: if the Empire falls, you *lose* for being
closest to the Lord Ruler. Cagey players keep scores close until the ending is clear.

**Tiebreak:** most resources in personal supply; still tied → shared victory.

## Components

- Board with a **Problem track** (Columns I–IV + "Problems Erupt" space) and an
  **Unrest track** (0–8+, marked by the Kelsier pawn).
- **Problem deck** (includes the special **Vin** card and **The Lord Ruler** card).
- **Personality deck** (+ 22-card *Great Houses* expansion, marked with an icon).
- **Favor / Disgrace / Ruined** tokens; **Inquisitor pawn** (Active Player marker).
- **House sheets** (Steel Ministry, Venture, Hasting, Elariel, Tekiel, Lekal, …).
- **Resource tokens**: Atium (wild), Food, Money, Prestige, Skaa, Warriors.

## Setup

- Unrest marker on 0.
- **Problem deck**: set Vin aside; split rest into 4 piles; shuffle Vin into one
  pile and set it aside; stack the other piles to set game length:
  - Short (60–90 min): 1 pile on top of Vin pile.
  - Medium (90–120): 2 piles on top.
  - Long (120–180): 3 piles on top. (Remaining piles go under.)
- Shuffle Personality deck.
- Resource supply by player count: **3p** 6 Atium + 8 each other; **4p** 7 + 10;
  **5p** 8 + 12.
- Deal each player a random **House sheet**; they collect the resources and draw
  the Personality cards shown on the sheet edges.
- **Starting Problems**: draw 1 per player; place at its starting Urgency column
  (slide right if full).
- **3-player games: the bottom row is unavailable** (max 2 Problems per column).
- First Active Player = House highest on the fixed priority list (Steel Ministry
  first). They take the Inquisitor pawn.

## Resources & Conversion

Six resources: **Atium, Food, Money, Prestige, Skaa, Warriors**. When paying any
cost you may convert (as often as you like, but never pooling with another
player mid-conversion):

- **1 Atium → any 1 resource** (Atium is wild).
- **Any 2 resources → any 1 resource**.

If the supply lacks a resource you're collecting, you may **steal** the shortfall
from other players (same resource types you'd normally collect). If nobody has
it (e.g. destroyed), the balance is simply lost. **Destroyed** resources are out
of play; **Ruined** icons can't be collected until the Ruined token is removed.

## Turn Structure (Active Player only)

1. **Collect Resources & Cards** — take the resources / draw the Personality
   cards on your House sheet (only you). **First turn = double.**
2. **Problems Worsen** — move *every* Problem 1 column right, one at a time, in
   an order you choose. Filling a column forces later Problems to skip it. A
   Problem pushed past Column IV **erupts** immediately (apply effect, discard).
   Multiple can erupt in a turn. Move-ordering is a core tactical lever.
3. **Add New Problem(s)** — draw 1 Problem to its starting Urgency column. If
   only one Problem is on the board afterward, draw another (always ≥ 2 on board).
4. **Pass or Deal** (choose one):
   - **Pass** → put pawn on "Pass the Turn"; take 1 resource (any, incl. Atium)
     *or* draw 1 Personality. Turn ends.
   - **Deal** → place the pawn on one Problem to open negotiation (see below).
5. **End Turn** — pawn passes left; next player becomes Active.

## Problem Cards

Features: **Starting Urgency** (Roman numeral = starting column), **Resource
Cost** (to solve), **Favor Reward** (split via the deal), **Rules** text
("When solved…"), **Eruption** effect (after the volcano icon), **Traits**
(base game: **REBEL**, **STEEL MINISTRY**), flavor text.

- A Problem's **Urgency always equals its current column.** Moving changes Urgency.
- **Soother** cards lower Urgency (move left); dropping below Column I puts the
  Problem back on **top of the deck** (drawn next).
- **Highest/lowest possible Urgency** = rightmost/leftmost available track space.
- **The Lord Ruler** card: cannot be solved; while in play **all Favor and
  Disgrace are doubled** (applied before other modifiers).
- **Vin**: when solved *or* erupts, the game ends; adds 4 Unrest on eruption.

## Eruptions

When a Problem passes Column IV, apply its eruption effect (Active Player chooses
any targets), then discard it. Common effects: **Add Problem(s)**, **Add Unrest**
(≥ 8 ends the game), **Destroy Resources** (→ Destroyed area), **Discard Cards**,
**"Each House…"** (each player chooses for themselves), **Gain Disgrace**,
**Return Resources to supply**, **Ruin a Resource** (place a Ruined token on any
resource icon on a House sheet / Personality in play).

## Deals (Solving a Problem)

Triggered when the Active Player places the pawn on a Problem in Step 4.

- You must pick the Problem with **no input** from others (pre-negotiation of
  *which* Problem is disallowed; players may scheme among themselves beforehand).
- The Active Player **controls the deal**, is always part of it, and may exclude
  any players. **No one can be forced** into a deal. Others typically offer
  resources toward the cost in exchange for a share of the Favor.
- **Offerable:** resources (incl. planned conversions), playing a Personality
  card, promises of future actions/choices, or anything the group's House Rules
  allow. **Bribes** (giving resources not needed for the cost) are allowed.
- **Never offerable:** scoring tokens (Favor/Disgrace stay with their owner);
  Personality cards already in play; Personality cards as bribes.
- **Showing hands:** you may reveal cards (must show *everyone*); you can ask to
  see a card (they may decline) — only a **Seeker** can force/privately view a hand.
- **If negotiation fails:** Problem stays put, committed resources return, played
  Personalities are still discarded, and (if it was your Step-4 choice) your turn
  ends — you can't pick another Problem.

### Closing a Deal (order)

The Active Player puts a hand back on the pawn, counts to five aloud, lifts it
(the **final call**). After that, only **Sly** cards may be played. Then:

1. **Pay the Resource Cost** — total paid must match the card's icons; apply
   cost-raising/lowering cards (Tineye, Thug, Lesser Noble, Informants); convert
   as needed.
2. **Apply "When Solved" effects** (Active Player chooses targets).
3. **Apply "Sly" Personality effects** (card's controller chooses targets).
4. **Award Favor** — each participant takes their agreed Favor, **face down**
   (scoring tokens are secret, never bartered; may swap for change openly).
5. **Discard the solved Problem.** Turn ends.

### Deal Binding

Terms are binding **only for the current turn** — promises extending to future
turns can be reneged **unless** an **Obligator** or **High Prelan** is in play
(makes all terms binding; failure → you discard 1 resource, or 2 if High Prelan
is Allied to Steel Ministry).

## Personality Cards

Not resources (can't be destroyed/ruined). Each states **when** it can be played
(most: "at any time"). Resolved in play order; simultaneous → clockwise from
Active Player. Deck runs out → reshuffle discards.

**Attributes:** **LASTING** (stays in play, ongoing/end-game effect; face up =
active effect, face down = scoring) · **SLY** (may be played *after* the final
call, resolved in Close step 3).

**Key card types & interactions:**
- **Soother** — lower a Problem's Urgency by I.
- **Advisors** (Lasting) — collect 1 extra resource each turn.
- **Tineye / Thug / Lesser Noble / Mistborn** — lower a Problem's cost;
  **Informants** raise it (can scuttle deals). Effects last until end of turn.
- **Coinshot vs. Hazekiller** — both discard other Personalities: **Coinshot**
  can only discard **Lasting** cards; **Hazekiller** can only discard
  **non-Lasting** cards.
- **Smoker** — reaction card; **redirects** a Personality or eruption effect to a
  new valid target (whole effect, unmodified). Cannot redirect "When Solved" effects.
- **Seeker** — look at a hand and steal a card (target can't pre-empt).
- **Con Artist** (Sly) — after helping solve, take *all* the Favor from that deal.
- **Slanderer** (Sly) — a player's gained Favor becomes equal Disgrace instead.
- **Lurcher** — steal resources / retrieve destroyed resources.
- **Obligator / High Prelan** (Lasting, Sly) — make deal terms binding.
- **Heirs / Honor Hostages** — played face down, revealed at game end to affect score.
- **Allies** — a Personality with an Ally tag matching your House may use its
  stronger **Ally** text instead.

**Great Houses expansion (22 cards):** each has the **Ally** trait (stronger for
its matching House). Add all of them, or only those allied to Houses in play.

## Strategy Notes

Control the **order Problems move** to make them erupt on your (or an ally's)
turn, where you direct the targets; fill columns to skip Problems past them; use
Soothers to stall dangerous eruptions. Deal constantly — small, repeated Favor
adds up. Don't lead too visibly: both the social table and a possible Empire
collapse punish an obvious front-runner.
